Early Life

Avani Chaturvedi was born on October 27, 1993, in Rewa, Madhya Pradesh. She completed her schooling from Adarsh Higher Secondary School in 2010, and went on to study engineering at Banasthali University.

Career

Chaturvedi joined the Indian Air Force in 2016, and was one of three women to be the first to be commissioned into the fighter stream of the IAF in 2018. She was trained on the MiG-21 Bison, and later transitioned to the MiG-21 Bison Type 96, an upgraded version of the aircraft.

She flew solo on a MiG-21 Bison on February 19, 2018, becoming the first Indian woman to do so. She also participated in the Republic Day fly-past in 2018.

Achievements

Chaturvedi's achievement of becoming the first Indian woman to fly a fighter jet solo was widely covered in the media, and she received congratulatory messages from across the country. She was also felicitated by the Chief of Air Staff for her achievement.

Chaturvedi was featured in the Forbes 30 under 30 Asia 2019 list in the category of entertainment and sports.

Legacy

Chaturvedi's achievement has been hailed as a significant step forward for gender equality in India. Her success has inspired many young girls to pursue their dreams, and has encouraged more women to join the Indian Air Force.
